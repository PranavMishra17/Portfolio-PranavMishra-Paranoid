PROMPT PATROL

A mouse-only, single-page, endless-runner where the player defends an LLM from rising adversarial prompts using projectile-shot pops under wind and gravity. Lives are the lifeline; high score is the bragging right.

01

TL;DR & Pitch

A user-mode keyboard at the bottom-right of the screen types prompts; the prompts rise as glowing word-capsules toward an LLM that hovers at the top of the play area. A cannon at the bottom-left fires projectiles in a gravity arc, deflected by a slow, ever-shifting wind. The player must pop the unsafe prompts before they reach the model. Letting ten unsafe prompts through ends the round. Score = unsafe prompts popped, moderated by penalties for popping safe ones.

Why this works

It's a one-button game (mouse aim + click), the input volume scales to any session length, and the theme is meta enough to be funny without needing a tutorial. It runs at 60fps on a canvas with <30 entities.

02

What does a prompt look like?

The user asked the right question — a balloon is wrong because a prompt isn't a balloon. A prompt is a fragment of text fed to a model. Three candidate visual metaphors were considered:

  • Speech bubble — accurate but visually noisy; the tail breaks the shape silhouette and makes hit-testing fiddly.
  • Glowing orb — clean but reads as generic projectile / collectible; loses the "this is text" signal.
  • Pill-shaped capsule with text inside ← chosen.
Picked: token capsules

Each prompt is a rounded-rectangle capsule with 6–14 characters of text inside, colored by safety class. Capsules read immediately as "text fragment" — they look like tokens in a tokenizer output, or chat fragments rising out of an input box. Stable silhouette for hit detection, easy to scale text legibility, and lets the joke layer carry through the text itself.

03

Play-field layout

One screen, no scrolling. Cannon fires from the bottom-left, capsules rise from the bottom-right. The model sits at the top — capsules that reach it cost a life. A wind flag in the upper-middle band shows current wind direction at all times.

cannon fires bottom-left → projectile arcs under wind → pops capsules rising from bottom-right keyboard toward model

The model frame at the top has a glow-spill underneath: when an unsafe token touches it, the glow flashes red and a life is consumed. The keyboard at the bottom-right faintly "presses keys" each time a new capsule spawns. Both are framing pixel art — they do not block gameplay but they sell the narrative.

Sizing rule

Capsule height stays fixed (24 px on desktop, 28 px on mobile). Width scales with text but capped at 130 px. Text uses Space Mono 11–13 px, weight 700, monospace so glyph width is predictable. Hit-test is the capsule's axis-aligned bounding rect.

05

Cannon, aim & the dashed indicator

The cannon is a fixed pixel-art turret anchored at (64, H-64) (bottom-left, padded). The barrel rotates to point at the mouse.

A short dashed segment extends from the barrel tip only ~120 px along the aim vector — not the full predicted parabola. The player sees the initial direction, not the answer. The line ends in a small open ring. The reasoning: showing the full trajectory turns the game into a calculation exercise; showing only direction keeps it a feel-the-arc challenge.

Aim spec
  • Aim vector = (mouse - cannonTip), normalized.
  • Barrel sprite rotates by atan2 of that vector.
  • Indicator dashes: 5 px on / 5 px off, length 120 px, opacity 0.7.
  • Click = launch one projectile at fixed launch speed (~720 px/s). No charging, no aim assist.
  • Rate limit: 250 ms cooldown between shots — visualized by the barrel briefly dimming.
06

Projectile physics

Each projectile is a 6×6 pixel chevron ▸ in CRT green with a thin tracer trail (4 last positions, alpha-fading). One update tick per frame:

Per-frame integration
  • vx += windAx · dt
  • vy += gravity · dt  (gravity ≈ 620 px/s²)
  • x += vx · dt; y += vy · dt
  • Despawn if y > H + 24 or x < -24 or x > W + 24.
  • Hit test against every active capsule's bbox. First hit pops, projectile dies.

Projectiles do not collide with each other and pass through the wind flag, keyboard, and cannon sprites. They do visually clip when they cross behind the model frame so the player can see when a shot was wildly off.

07

Wind system

Wind is always horizontal. Vertical drift is reserved for the rising capsules themselves. Three magnitudes, never stronger:

Wind levels
  • Still · ax = 0 px/s² — flag droops.
  • Light · ax = ±18 px/s² — flag waves gently.
  • Moderate · ax = ±42 px/s² — flag stretches taut.

A wind state persists 20–30 seconds (uniform random in that range), then transitions over 1.5 s to a new state sampled with these weights: still 25 %, light-L 22.5 %, light-R 22.5 %, mod-L 15 %, mod-R 15 %. The transition is linear; players see the flag slowly rotate to the new orientation.

Hard rule

Wind never exceeds moderate. Players must feel the wind but never feel cheated by it. If gameplay testing shows a strong wind exists in any path, it's a bug.

08

Spawn pattern & pacing

Endless ≠ purely random. The session is a sequence of 20-second waves. Each wave is one of four named templates that gets slightly perturbed per instance:

Wave templates
  • Calm — 6 capsules. Distribution: 5 safe, 1 unsafe, 1 chance of gold. 3.5 s avg spacing.
  • Mixed — 10 capsules. 50/50 safe/unsafe, ~10% gold, ~5% bomb. 2 s avg spacing.
  • Rush — 16 capsules. 70% unsafe, 30% safe, 1 bomb guaranteed. 1.2 s avg spacing.
  • Recovery — 8 capsules. 4 safe, 2 unsafe, 1 gold, 1 egg-candidate. 2.5 s avg spacing.

Wave order follows a fixed rhythm: Calm → Mixed → Rush → Recovery and loops. Each loop iteration applies a +4 % spawn rate and +2 % rise speed bump, capped at +40 %. The loop and the wave both seed off the session start timestamp so two players starting at the same minute see comparable sessions — important for the leaderboard.

Within a wave, capsule classes are drawn at the wave's distribution but kept locally balanced via a small shuffle bag so the player never gets, say, six unsafes in a row by chance.

09

Lives, scoring & game over

Score

  • Unsafe popped: +1
  • Safe popped: -1
  • Gold popped: +5
  • Egg popped: +10
  • Bomb popped: -3, -1 life

Lives

  • Start: 10 ❤
  • Unsafe escapes top: -1 life
  • Bomb popped: -1 life
  • Game over at 0.

Game over

  • Freeze the world; cannon fades.
  • Score, wave reached, accuracy.
  • 3-char initials entry.
  • Submit → leaderboard.
Streak bonus (optional, see features list)

Five consecutive unsafe pops with zero safe-pops in between activates a 2× score multiplier that decays on next safe-pop or 10-s timer expiry. Visualized as the cannon glowing brighter.

HUD & framing pixel art

Three pixel-art elements anchor the frame. Each is small (≤ 96 × 64 px), drawn directly to canvas or via SVG sprites, and exists to sell the narrative without grabbing focus from gameplay.

MODEL
CRT face with cycling output. Glow flashes red when hit.
WIND FLAG
Pennant rotates between three orientations; never stronger than moderate.
KEYBOARD
Keys depress briefly each spawn. Tokens "type up" from here.
CANNON
Barrel rotates to mouse. Dims for 250 ms after firing.
HUD overlay (top corners)
  • Top-left: 10 hearts arrayed horizontally; deplete on unsafe escape / bomb pop.
  • Top-right: SCORE in Press Start 2P, big and crisp. Below it, WAVE counter in Space Mono.
  • No text instructions during play — onboarding is a 4-second title screen "AIM · CLICK · POP UNSAFE".

12

Leaderboard hook

The full no-backend leaderboard plan already lives at the top of portfolio/src/components/MiniGame.js. Summary: Firebase Realtime DB (Spark free tier) primary, getpantry.cloud fallback. Reads via onValue for live updates. Writes constrained by security rules to {name: 3 chars, score: int 0–10000, ts: int}.

Submission flow at game-over: 3-character initials entry (arcade style), submit button, then animate the new entry sliding into place on the top-10 panel. Personal best stays in localStorage (pp_personal_best_v1) and renders above the global board.

Feature / idea / bounds list

Everything I considered, organized so you can pick what ships. Numbered so you can reply with "do 03, 07, 12, skip the rest".

Core — v1 must-have

  1. Cannon, mouse-aim, dashed direction indicator.
  2. One-button projectile launch with cooldown.
  3. Projectile physics: gravity + wind, fixed launch speed.
  4. Wind state machine with persistent 20–30 s phases and visible flag.
  5. Capsule spawn from bottom-right keyboard, upward drift.
  6. Two classes (safe / unsafe) shipping at start.
  7. Hit detection & pop animation.
  8. 10 lives + score + game over.
  9. Pixel-art model, cannon, keyboard, flag.
  10. Title screen + game-over screen.

Polish — v1.1 quality of life

  1. Particle shatter on pop (8–12 mini-pixels).
  2. Projectile tracer trail (4-frame fade).
  3. Subtle screen-shake (≤ 4 px) on heart-loss.
  4. Wind-change banner: "WIND ▸ MODERATE" for 1.5 s.
  5. Wave-milestone banner every 5 waves.
  6. Mutable SFX: pop, miss, life-loss, gold, game-over.
  7. 3-character initials entry on game over.
  8. Personal-best line above leaderboard.
  9. Combo streak meter with 2× multiplier.
  10. Cannon barrel dim for cooldown ms.
  11. prefers-reduced-motion kills shake, sparks, and tracer.

Stretch — later

  1. Gold token class (+5).
  2. Bomb token class (-3, -1 life).
  3. Easter-egg tokens (+10, confetti).
  4. Daily seed: same RNG for everyone on the same calendar day.
  5. Power-up: freeze-time (3 s) every N pops.
  6. Power-ups: wider shot, double-shot.
  7. Replay system: store last 60 s of inputs.
  8. Spectate top run on idle title screen.
  9. Customizable cannon skin (3 unlockables).
  10. Achievement set (15–20 small ones).
  11. "Storm" event: ~1 % chance per wave, wind goes loud-but-still-fair for 8 s with visual storm overlay.
  12. Calibration mode (practice, no lives).
  13. Speedrun timer to N safe escapes.

Bounds — honest limits

  1. Mobile: mouse → touch. Tokens get +40 % hit area so fingers work.
  2. Touch can aim by drag or tap-and-fire; tap-and-fire is faster but less precise. Pick one.
  3. Bundle: target ≤ 18 KB gzip game JS (no game engine, plain canvas).
  4. Performance: max 24 capsules + 12 projectiles + 60 particles on screen before degradation.
  5. Capsule text ≤ 14 chars. Longer phrases get truncated.
  6. Wind never above moderate. Hard rule, no exceptions.
  7. Pop penalty for safe pops is real and felt — game can't degrade to "click everything".
  8. Free leaderboard backend has rate limits — throttle submissions to one per game-over.
  9. Music: not shipping. Adds 100+ KB and licensing risk.
  10. No accounts. Initials only. No PII.
  11. Easter eggs use Pranav-specific content — anyone is welcome to find them but they're not signposted.
Recommended v1 cut

Ship Core 01–10 plus Polish 01–04, 07–08, 11. Defer everything else. That's a single-session build, ~500 lines of code, ~12 KB gzipped, with the joke layer carried entirely by the phrasebook (which is free to expand later without code changes).
